Bottom Line

Apple is projected to overtake Samsung as the world's top smartphone vendor in 2025 for the first time since 2011, with Counterpoint Research forecasting 19.4% market share vs. Samsung's 18.7% driven by 10% YoY shipment growth vs. Samsung's 4.6-5%. This outlook is supported by strong iPhone 17 demand, particularly in China where Apple captured 26% share during Singles' Day and single-handedly drove the market's 3% sales growth, while Huawei fell to 13% from 17% and Xiaomi held 17% but saw sales decline per Counterpoint. The stock reached $4.12 trillion valuation as JPMorgan noted increased CEO succession clarity. However, Apple faces a significant legal challenge in India over a new antitrust law that could impose penalties up to $38B (34% of FY2025's $112B net income per 10-K), with a hearing scheduled for December 3 per Reuters. (Nov 26-27)
 
Major Developments

India Antitrust Law ChallengeBearish

Apple filed a 545-page legal challenge in the Delhi High Court against India's new antitrust penalty law, which allows the Competition Commission of India (CCI) to levy fines based on global turnover, potentially reaching $38B for Apple (representing ~34% of the company's $112B FY2025 net income per 10-K). The company argues penalties should be based only on local revenue from the specific business unit in violation, calling the global turnover basis "manifestly arbitrary" and unconstitutional. The challenge stems from an ongoing CCI case where investigators found alleged "abusive conduct" related to Apple's iOS app market management per Reuters. The FY2025 10-K details multiple ongoing antitrust cases, including with the EU (DMA) and US (DOJ), indicating this is part of a broader regulatory landscape. (Nov 27)

Future iPhone RoadmapBullish

Apple plans to introduce three new iPhone models within the next three years and is altering its release schedule, in what is described as a major transformation for the device's history per Benzinga. Bloomberg reports Apple is preparing several high-impact products for 2026 and 2027, including a potential iPhone Fold, the iPhone 17e, and a special 20th-anniversary iPhone, expected to maintain sales momentum per Batam News Asia. The iPhone represents 50% of total net sales ($209.6B of $416.2B) in FY2025 per the 10-K, with management calling the current lineup the "strongest ever" on the Oct 30 call, driving record Q4 revenue and guiding for double-digit growth. (Nov 26-27)
Financial / Operational Performance

Stock Hits All-Time High

Apple's stock reached a new all-time high of $280.38, pushing its market capitalization to $4.12 trillion. The stock has gained over 11% in 2025, 18-19% over the last 12 months, and 21.2% over the past three months, outpacing the Dow Jones index per multiple reports. The company maintains an annual dividend yield of 0.38%, paying a quarterly dividend of 26 cents per share confirmed on the Oct 30 call. Per the FY2025 10-K, Apple returned $89.3B via share repurchases during the fiscal year and intends to increase its dividend annually; the company generated $112B in net income in FY2025. (Nov 26)

Q3 Shipment Growth

Apple's smartphone shipments posted 9% year-over-year growth during the third quarter, according to data from Counterpoint Research. This growth contributes to the firm's upgraded forecast for Apple to lead the global market in 2025 per multiple reports. While Apple does not report unit shipments, for its fiscal Q3 2025 (ended June 28), the company reported iPhone net sales of $44.6B, a 13.45% year-over-year increase per the 10-Q, corroborating the strong growth reported by the third-party research firm. (Nov 26)
Risk Factors

TSMC Trade Secret Probe

A potential supply chain and intellectual property risk has emerged as key supplier TSMC filed a lawsuit against a former senior vice president, Wei-Jen Lo, for allegedly leaking trade secrets to Intel, where Lo is now employed. Taiwanese prosecutors have raided Lo's home in connection with the probe. Lo was instrumental in TSMC's development of 2-nm to 5-nm chips, and any compromise of this technology could impact the technological leadership Apple relies on for its processors per Yahoo Finance. Apple's FY2025 10-K discloses a material dependency on single or limited-source component suppliers located primarily in Asia; the company's AI strategy, per the Oct 30 call, is heavily reliant on its custom Apple Silicon chips manufactured by these key partners. (Nov 27)
Wall Street Sentiment

JPMorgan on Succession

JPMorgan analyst Samik Chatterjee stated that increased clarity on CEO succession is a key positive for investors, suggesting the market should be positioning for the leadership transition to hardware chief John Ternus. Chatterjee noted this clarity helps resolve a key investor fixation and unlocks a new chapter for the company focused on a hardware-first, AI-supercharged future per Benzinga. (Nov 26)

Analyst Estimates Raised

Wall Street analysts have increased Apple's revenue estimates for the current quarter by 10.4%, reflecting positive market confidence in the company's performance. This optimism is partly fueled by expectations of strong holiday sales driven by promotions from partners like T-Mobile per Pintu. (Nov 26)

Cramer on iPhone 17 and AI

Jim Cramer described the new iPhone 17 as a "huge bargain" considering trade-ins and subsidies, stating, "As long as Apple makes the best products, people will buy them." The CNBC Investing Club maintains its "own, don't trade" thesis on the stock, and despite calling Apple's AI strategy "lackluster," it views the company as uniquely positioned to benefit from AI by monetizing its large installed base through partnerships per CNBC. (Nov 26)
Competitive / Industry Dynamics

[Competitor] Projected to Overtake Samsung in 2025Bullish

Counterpoint Research forecasts Apple will surpass Samsung in annual smartphone shipments in 2025 for the first time since 2011, achieving 19.4% global market share versus Samsung's 18.7%. Apple's iPhone shipments are projected to grow 10% YoY in 2025, outpacing Samsung's 4.6-5% growth and the overall market's 3.3% rise. Counterpoint expects Apple to maintain this leadership position through 2029 per multiple reports. While the FY2025 10-K notes Apple has a minority share in the global smartphone market, management cited a survey on the Oct 30 call showing iPhone as the top-selling model in key markets like the U.S. and Urban China; iPhone revenue grew 4% in FY2025 to $209.6B. (Nov 26-27)

[Market] Dominance in China's Singles' DayBullish

During China's month-long Singles' Day shopping festival, Apple captured 26% of all smartphones sold, single-handedly driving the market's total 3% YoY sales growth. Excluding Apple, smartphone sales declined 5%, reflecting weak consumer demand for rival brands amid economic caution. Competitor Huawei's share fell to 13% from 17% last year, while Xiaomi held a 17% share but saw sales decline per Counterpoint. Greater China represented 15.5% of total net sales ($64.4B) in FY2025, though sales declined 4% YoY per the 10-K; on the Oct 30 call, CEO Tim Cook stated he expects a "return to growth in Q1," citing significantly increased store traffic and strong iPhone reception. (Nov 27)

[Market] Smartphone Market Growth DriversBullish

Key drivers for projected smartphone market growth include a replacement cycle reaching an inflection point as consumers who bought devices during the COVID-19 boom begin to upgrade. Counterpoint Research also identified a sizable future demand base from the 358 million second-hand iPhones sold between 2023 and Q2 2025. Macroeconomic factors such as a truce in the US-China tech war and lower tariffs are also helping streamline supply chains and boost demand per multiple reports. On the Oct 30 call, CEO Tim Cook confirmed a "September quarter record for upgraders," supporting the thesis of an active replacement cycle; the FY2025 10-K identifies stimulating demand for new and upgraded products as a key element for continued success. (Nov 26-27)

[Downstream] Positive Demand Signals from Best BuyBullish

Key retail partner Best Buy reported a 2.4% YoY sales increase to $9.67B for its fiscal Q3, beating estimates, and raised its full-year guidance. The strong performance was driven by robust sales in computing, gaming, and mobile phones, with CEO Corie Barry specifically citing new iPhones as a factor encouraging upgrades. This indicates healthy consumer demand for Apple's products in the North American market per multiple reports. Indirect channels, including resellers like Best Buy, accounted for 60% of Apple's total net sales in FY2025 per the 10-K; the company's filings disclose a material dependency on these third-party resellers for product distribution and sales. (Nov 26)

[Competitor] Samsung Partners on On-Device AINeutral

Samsung Electronics entered a technology collaboration with Nota AI to integrate AI model optimization technology into its Exynos AI Studio. This partnership aims to enhance the performance of on-device generative AI on Samsung's latest Exynos 2500 application processor, enabling advanced AI experiences on smartphones without cloud reliance. This move signals a key strategic focus for a primary competitor in the smartphone AI race per PR Newswire. This competitive move directly targets Apple's core AI strategy; on its Oct 30 call, Apple management detailed its own focus on "on-device foundation models" to power its new "Apple Intelligence" features, enabled by its proprietary A19 Pro and M5 chips. (Nov 26)
